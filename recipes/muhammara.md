### Ingredients

- 3 large red bell peppers
- 1 cup walnuts, lightly toasted
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
- 1 tsp dried Aleppo pepper or mild red chili flakes
- 2 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp olive oil, plus more to finish
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 slice day-old bread, torn (optional, for body)

### Steps

#### Roast and peel the peppers
Char the bell peppers directly over a gas flame or under a broiler, turning occasionally, until completely blackened on all sides — about 10–15 minutes. Place in a bowl, cover with a plate, and let steam for 15 minutes. Peel off the charred skin and remove seeds and stems. Don't rinse — a little char is flavor.

#### Toast the walnuts
In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the walnuts until fragrant and lightly golden, about 4 minutes. Watch carefully — walnuts burn fast. Let cool slightly.

#### Blend
Add the walnuts to a food processor and pulse until they form a coarse crumble. Add the roasted peppers, pomegranate molasses, Aleppo pepper, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, and salt. If using bread, add it now — it gives more body and a slightly smoother texture. Pulse until combined but still textured. It should not be completely smooth.

#### Balance and rest
Taste carefully. Muhammara lives in the balance of sweet (pomegranate molasses), savory (walnut, garlic), and tangy (lemon). Add more pomegranate molasses for sweetness, lemon for brightness, salt if flat, or chili for heat. Rest 20 minutes before serving — it opens up as it sits.

### Notes

Pomegranate molasses is the irreplaceable ingredient here — find it at Middle Eastern grocers or online. Aleppo pepper is also worth seeking out; it has a fruity, mild heat that differs from regular chili flakes. To serve: spread in a wide shallow bowl, drizzle generously with olive oil, scatter a few whole toasted walnuts and a pinch of Aleppo on top. Keeps refrigerated for a week. Gets better on day two.
