### Ingredients

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- ½ cup walnuts, finely chopped
- 1½ cups vegetable broth
- 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and diced small (½-inch cubes)
- ½ cup brown or green lentils, rinsed
- 1 can black beans (15 oz), drained
- 1 can kidney beans (15 oz), drained
- 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz), do not drain
- 1 can pumpkin puree (15 oz)
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- salt and black pepper to taste

### Steps

#### Sauté
Hit Sauté on the Instant Pot. Heat the olive oil, then cook the onion and bell pepper for 3 minutes. Add the garlic and walnuts, stirring constantly for 1 minute until fragrant and the walnuts are lightly toasted.

#### Deglaze
Pour in a splash of vegetable broth. Use a wooden spoon to scrape every bit of food off the bottom of the pot — this prevents a Burn warning. Turn off Sauté.

#### Layer (order matters)
Pour in the remaining broth, sweet potato, and lentils. Stir just these bottom ingredients. Layer the black beans, kidney beans, and diced tomatoes on top. Finally, spoon the pumpkin puree and all the spices on the very top. Do not stir — layering keeps the starchy ingredients off the bottom.

#### Pressure cook
Lock the lid and set the valve to Sealing. Cook on Manual / High Pressure for 15 minutes. The extra time ensures lentils and sweet potatoes are perfectly tender.

#### Natural release
Allow a 10-minute natural pressure release, then carefully vent any remaining steam manually.

#### The scoopable finish
Open the lid and stir thoroughly to blend the pumpkin throughout. For even more body, use a potato masher to mash a portion of the beans and sweet potato directly in the pot. Let sit 5 minutes before serving — it thickens significantly as it cools slightly.

### Notes

Toppings are half the point with this one: thick vegan sour cream or coconut yogurt, diced avocado, diced red onion or pickled jalapeños, and tortilla chips or warm cornbread for scooping. The walnuts give a surprisingly meaty texture — don't skip them. Leftovers thicken even further overnight; thin with a splash of broth when reheating.
