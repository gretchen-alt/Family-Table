### Ingredients

- 1 cup raw pepitas (hulled pumpkin seeds)
- 4 roma tomatoes
- ½ white onion, cut into thick slices
- 2 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- 1 habanero or serrano pepper (use half for mild)
- ½ bunch cilantro, roughly chopped
- 2 limes, juiced
- ½ tsp salt

### Steps

#### Char the vegetables
Heat a dry cast iron skillet or heavy pan over high heat until very hot. Add the tomatoes, onion, garlic, and pepper directly to the dry pan. Char without moving them until blackened and blistered on one side, then turn — about 10 minutes total. The tomatoes should be collapsing and deeply charred in spots. Peel the garlic once cool enough to handle.

#### Toast the pepitas
In the same pan over medium heat, toast the pepitas dry, shaking frequently, until they pop and turn golden — about 4–5 minutes. Watch carefully; they go from golden to burned quickly. Let cool slightly.

#### Blend
Add the toasted pepitas to a blender or food processor and pulse until they become a coarse meal — not a paste. Add the charred tomatoes, onion, garlic, and pepper (remove habanero seeds if you want less heat). Pulse until combined but still textured. It should be thick and a little chunky, not smooth.

#### Finish and season
Transfer to a bowl. Stir in the cilantro and lime juice. Season with salt. Taste — it should be smoky, nutty, bright, and have some heat. Add more lime if it needs lifting. Let it rest 10 minutes before serving so the flavors settle.

### Notes

This is traditionally served at room temperature, not warm. The char is non-negotiable — it's what makes it taste ancient rather than just a green dip. A cast iron pan is ideal. With garden tomatoes in summer, this recipe becomes extraordinary. Keeps in the fridge 4–5 days.
