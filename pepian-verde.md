### Ingredients

- 1 cup raw pumpkin seeds (pepitas), hulled
- ¼ cup sesame seeds
- 4 roma tomatoes
- 2 tomatillos, husked
- 2 dried ancho or guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- ½ white onion, roughly chopped
- 3 garlic cloves
- 1½ cups vegetable broth
- ½ tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lb zucchini or chayote, cubed
- 1 cup chickpeas, cooked (or one 14 oz can, drained)

### Steps

#### Toast seeds and chiles
In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the pepitas until they pop and turn golden, about 5 minutes. Set aside. Toast the sesame seeds until golden, 2–3 minutes. Set aside separately. Toast the dried chiles briefly, 30 seconds per side, until fragrant. Soak chiles in hot water for 15 minutes to soften.

#### Char the vegetables
Place the tomatoes, tomatillos, onion, and garlic on a baking sheet or directly in a cast iron pan. Char under the broiler or over a gas flame until blackened in spots, about 8–10 minutes. This char is essential — it creates the smoky depth of the sauce.

#### Blend the sauce
Blend the pepitas, sesame seeds, and cumin with the vegetable broth until very smooth. Add the charred vegetables and soaked chiles and blend again until silky. Add a splash more broth if needed.

#### Fry the sauce
Heat olive oil in a large pot. Pour in the blended sauce carefully — it will sputter. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, for 10 minutes until the sauce darkens slightly and thickens. Season with salt.

#### Add vegetables and simmer
Add the zucchini and chickpeas to the pot. Simmer 15–20 minutes until vegetables are tender and sauce coats everything. Taste and adjust salt. Serve with a handful of toasted pepitas on top.

### Notes

This is wonderful over roasted cauliflower, sweet potato, or any sturdy vegetable. A handful of cilantro at serving lifts the whole dish. The sauce freezes beautifully — make a double batch.
