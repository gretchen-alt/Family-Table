### Ingredients

- 300 g navy beans, dry (unsoaked)
- 200 g pinto beans, dry (unsoaked)
- 6 cups water (for pre-cook)
- 75 g bacon or smoked pancetta, diced
- 1 yellow onion, finely diced
- ½ red bell pepper, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 3 tbsp ketchup
- 3 tbsp unsulfured molasses
- 2 tbsp brown sugar, packed
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
- ½ cup water (for sauce cook)
- 1 tsp salt, to taste

### Steps

#### Pre-cook the beans
Add the navy beans and pinto beans to the Instant Pot with 6 cups of water. Secure the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and pressure cook on HIGH for 20 minutes. This softens the beans and draws out compounds that cause digestive discomfort.

#### Quick release and rinse
Carefully quick release the pressure. Drain the beans into a colander and rinse well under cold running water. Set aside. Rinse out the Instant Pot insert and wipe clean — fresh start for the sauce.

#### Sauté bacon and aromatics
Set the Instant Pot to Sauté. Add the bacon and cook until the fat renders and edges just start to crisp, about 3–4 minutes. Add the onion and red bell pepper and cook until softened, another 3 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.

#### Build the sauce
Stir in the tomato paste, ketchup, molasses, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste darkens slightly. Do not add salt yet — it toughens beans under pressure.

#### Combine and deglaze
Add the rinsed beans back to the pot. Pour in the stock and water. Stir everything together, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom — this is important to prevent a Burn warning.

#### Pressure cook
Secure the lid and set the valve to Sealing. Pressure cook on HIGH for 25 minutes.

#### Natural release and finish
Allow a full natural release (about 20 minutes). Open the lid and stir. The sauce should be thick and glossy. If it needs more body, switch to Sauté and simmer uncovered for a few minutes. Add salt to taste now, stir, and let rest 5 minutes before serving.

### Notes

The red bell pepper adds vitamin C, which meaningfully improves absorption of the non-heme iron in the beans and molasses — a small nutritional win. Unsulfured molasses is worth seeking out; it contributes meaningful iron, calcium, magnesium, potassium, and manganese. Leftovers thicken considerably overnight — thin with a splash of stock when reheating. These freeze beautifully.
