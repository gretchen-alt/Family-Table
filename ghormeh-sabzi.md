### Ingredients

- 1 cup dried black-eyed peas or small white beans, soaked overnight
- 2 cups flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 1 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (or 2 tsp dried)
- 1 cup fresh chives or green onion tops, chopped
- 1 cup spinach or additional parsley
- 4 dried Persian limes (limu omani), pierced with a knife
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1½ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp ground black pepper
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 1 tsp salt

### Steps

#### Sauté the onion
In a wide pot or Instant Pot on sauté mode, cook the onion in olive oil over medium heat until deeply golden — about 15 minutes. Don't rush this; it's the flavor base. Add turmeric and black pepper and stir for 1 minute.

#### Fry the herbs
In a separate pan, heat a little oil and sauté the parsley, fenugreek, chives, and spinach together over medium-high heat, stirring frequently, until they darken significantly and reduce to about a third of their volume — 10–15 minutes. They should smell toasty and almost dry. This step is what gives ghormeh sabzi its distinctive depth. Don't skip it.

#### Combine and pressure cook
Add the sautéed herbs, drained beans, dried limes, and vegetable broth to the onion pot. If using an Instant Pot, seal and cook on High Pressure for 25 minutes, then natural release 15 minutes. On the stovetop, simmer covered for 1½–2 hours until beans are completely tender.

#### Finish and season
Remove the dried limes and squeeze their juice back into the stew — they'll be soft now. Add salt and taste. The stew should be deeply savory, herbaceous, and tangy. Add more lime juice if needed. Serve with yogurt on the side.

### Notes

Persian dried limes (limu omani) are the soul of this dish — find them at Middle Eastern grocery stores or online. They are irreplaceable. If you can't get them, use a combination of fresh lime juice and lime zest added at the end, though the flavor will be milder. Ghormeh sabzi tastes significantly better the next day.
