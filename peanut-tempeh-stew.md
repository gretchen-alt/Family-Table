### Ingredients

- 8 oz tempeh, cut into 1-inch cubes
- ½ cup natural peanut butter (no sugar)
- 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 1 sweet potato, cubed
- 2 large handfuls collard greens or kale, ribboned
- 1 tsp harissa or cayenne
- 2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp salt

### Steps

#### Marinate and sear the tempeh
Toss the tempeh with the tamari and a little olive oil and let sit 15–30 minutes if you have time. Pan-sear in olive oil over medium-high heat until golden on most sides, about 8 minutes. Set aside.

#### Build the base
In the same pan, sauté the onion until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger and cook 2 more minutes until fragrant.

#### Add peanut broth and vegetables
Whisk the peanut butter into the vegetable broth until smooth. Add this mixture, diced tomatoes, sweet potato, harissa, and the seared tempeh to the pot. Stir well.

#### Simmer
Simmer partially covered for 25–30 minutes until sweet potato is tender and stew has thickened. Stir frequently — peanut stews like to stick.

#### Finish with greens and acid
Add the collard greens and stir until just wilted, about 3 minutes. Stir in the apple cider vinegar. Taste and adjust with salt. Serve with lime wedges.

### Notes

The tempeh marinade step is optional but transforms it — the tamari gives deep umami that reads almost like meat. To make this purely bean-based, swap in 2 cups of cooked chickpeas added with the sweet potato. Instant Pot method: sauté onion, garlic, and ginger on sauté mode, add everything except greens, cook High Pressure 8 minutes, quick release, then stir in greens on sauté mode until wilted.
